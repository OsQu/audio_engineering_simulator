- **Make the keybed a composable widget, not an `isPlayable` heuristic.** Today App appends the on-screen keybed to a focus view whenever `isPlayable(desc)` — i.e. the device has *any* events-domain input (`App.svelte` ~1051 + the `keyboardTarget` derivation). That conflates "has a MIDI input" with "is a playable instrument": the 8i6's **MIDI-In is a thru** (MIDI In → `EventThru` → MIDI Out), so focusing it (since 5.7.6) wrongly shows a keybed whose notes route to MIDI-Out and make no sound. The right fix is to make `<Keybed>` a shared widget a focus surface **composes when it wants one** (the 5.7.3 registry ethos — no App-level flag), so the surface owns whether it has a keybed. Entails: (1) move note plumbing onto the `DeviceHandle` (`playNote`/`heldNotes`/`eventsInputOpen`); (2) make `<Keybed>` self-contained — attach/detach QWERTY capture on mount/unmount, register its device as the active note-sink so global Web-MIDI routes to *it* (not to `keyboardTarget`), retiring the `isPlayable` heuristic from note routing too; (3) give a surface a way to know it's rendering in focus (a `focus` prop on `DeviceUiProps`, analogous to `flipped`) since the synth's focus surface *is* its in-world faceplate and must not show a keybed in the rack. Consequence to accept: focusing the 8i6 will no longer auto-inject external MIDI into its thru (drive the thru from a patched controller instead). Moderate UI-architecture refactor; surfaced during Story 5.7 — tackle after it.
